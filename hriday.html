HRIDAY

Houston Research Initiative to Decipher Early Atherosclerosis and Drivers of Cardiometabolic Risk

Unlocking the Biological Code

HRIDAY will locally recruit approximately 1,000 young adults and use advanced analytic tools to decipher how genetic, social, and environmental risk factors contribute to early heart disease.

Program Overview

This prospective study is designed to understand early atherosclerosis and cardiometabolic risk before disease becomes clinically apparent.

The study will integrate community-informed recruitment with advanced imaging, biomarkers, and analytic methods.

Key Focus Areas

ParticipantsApproximately 1,000 young adults
BiologyGenetic and cardiometabolic drivers
ContextSocial and environmental risk factors

Help unlock the biological code of early heart disease.
